Project: Wind Tunnel for Airfoil Lift Measurements

Designed and built a small-scale wind tunnel to measure lift forces on model airfoils. Demonstrates CAD, fabrication, electronics, and testing skills.

Motivation & Context

Step-by-Step Build Log

Click each section to expand full project details.

1) Research & Planning

Researched designs, materials, costs, and calculated Reynolds number for laminar flow.

2) Material Selection & Costing

Selected plywood, sheet metal, straws, adhesives, and glass for budget and accessibility.

3) CAD Design & Drawings

Designed in SolidWorks and created dimensioned drawings for fabrication.

4) Cutting & Assembly

Cut plywood, joined with adhesive and nails, installed glass viewing panel, and added straw flow straighteners.

5) Strain Gauge & Electronics

Soldered strain gauge to HX711 amplifier, fabricated brackets, mounted pencil lever arm.

6) Arduino Coding & Calibration

Programmed Arduino to display lift data and calibrated the system.

7) Test Setup

Mounted airfoil on pencil lever, measured angles of attack, prepared fan airflow.

8) Testing & Data Collection

Ran fan, recorded lift at various angles via Arduino serial monitor.
