Wind Tunnel Project Portfolio

Mechanical Engineering – Lancaster University

Project Overview

This portfolio documents the design, construction, and testing of a small-scale wind tunnel for aerodynamic experiments.

Methodology

The wind tunnel was designed using CAD, fabricated from plywood and sheet metal, and instrumented with a strain gauge. Testing involved airflow measurements and lift analysis for a model airfoil.

Results & Analysis

Key results include velocity uniformity in the test section, calibration data, and lift measurements across different angles of attack. Graphs and plots can be added later.

Wind Tunnel Project — Step by Step

A curated build log showing the main decisions, fabrication stages, and testing steps.

1) Research & Planning

Conducted initial research on tunnel design requirements, materials, and costs. Performed Reynolds number calculations to ensure laminar flow conditions.

2) CAD Design

Designed the contraction, test section, and diffuser in SolidWorks. Created dimensioned drawings to guide fabrication.

3) Fabrication

Cut plywood panels with a circular saw, joined using adhesive and nails, and added a professionally cut glass viewing panel. Constructed flow straighteners with straws and spray adhesive.

4) Instrumentation & Strain Gauge Setup

Soldered a single-point load cell strain gauge to an HX711 amplifier. Machined aluminium sheet brackets and bolted them with M5×0.5 and M6×0.5 fasteners. Mounted a wooden block with a pencil as a lever arm for lift measurement.

5) Arduino Coding & Calibration

Programmed the Arduino IDE to display lift readings. Calibrated the load cell for accurate measurements.

6) Testing & Data Collection

Drilled a mounting point for the pencil, attached a cardboard airfoil with masking tape, measured lift at varying angles of attack, and analysed the data with the fan running.
